## Tax Invoice vs Invoice in Australia

In Australia, an invoice is just a payment request. A **tax invoice** is a specific document that allows the recipient to claim a GST credit (also called an "input tax credit") with the ATO. The distinction matters:

- **Regular invoice**: No GST shown, or sender is not registered for GST
- **Tax invoice**: GST is shown as a separate line; sender has an ABN and is registered for GST

### When must you issue a tax invoice?

If you are registered for GST, you must issue a tax invoice when:
- The sale is **$82.50 or more** (including GST)
- The buyer requests one

The buyer needs the tax invoice to claim their GST credit. If you don't provide it within 28 days of their request, you may be in breach of your GST obligations.

## ATO Tax Invoice Requirements

According to the ATO, a valid tax invoice for sales up to $1,000 must include:

| Field | Required |
|---|---|
| The words "Tax Invoice" (not just "Invoice") | Yes |
| Your business name | Yes |
| Your ABN (Australian Business Number) | Yes |
| Date the tax invoice was issued | Yes |
| Brief description of the goods or services | Yes |
| GST amount payable (either as a separate line, or a statement that "Total includes GST of $X") | Yes |
| Total price including GST | Yes |

For sales **over $1,000**, the invoice must also include:
- Buyer's identity or ABN
- Quantity or extent of what was sold

## Australian GST Rules at a Glance

- GST rate: **10%** (flat, no reduced rates)
- GST threshold: If your business turns over **$75,000 or more per year** ($150,000 for non-profits), you must register for GST
- Below the threshold: You can choose to register voluntarily, or not register and issue regular invoices without GST
- GST-free supplies: Basic food, some medical services, educational courses, exports — GST is 0% on these (not the same as not charging GST)

## Common Australian Invoicing Mistakes

**1. Writing "Invoice" instead of "Tax Invoice"**
If you're GST-registered and the sale is over $82.50, the document must say "Tax Invoice." A regular "Invoice" doesn't allow the recipient to claim GST credits.

**2. Missing ABN**
Your ABN must appear on every tax invoice. Without it, the invoice is non-compliant and your client may withhold 47% of the payment under the ATO's no-ABN withholding rules.

**3. Not showing GST separately**
GST must be identified — either as a separate line ("GST: $45.00") or a statement ("Price includes GST of $45.00"). Showing only a total doesn't meet ATO requirements.

**4. Charging GST when not registered**
You can only charge GST if you're registered for it. Charging GST without registration is illegal.

**5. Missing the date**
The invoice date is required. It's also the reference date for payment terms and for the recipient's BAS (Business Activity Statement) period.
