## What a resignation letter actually needs

A resignation letter is short by design. It only needs four things:

1. A clear statement that you're resigning
2. Your position and the company
3. Your last working day
4. A brief, courteous thank-you

That's it. You don't owe a detailed explanation, and you should never vent. The goal is a clean paper trail and a professional exit.

## Do you have to give a reason?

No. **A reason is never required.** You can leave it out entirely, or keep it to one neutral line like "I've accepted a new opportunity." The generator has an optional reason field — blank is perfectly fine, and often better.

## How much notice to give

Two weeks is the standard in the US for most roles, though your contract or local law may ask for more. The tool covers the common situations as preset types:

- **Two weeks' notice** — the standard
- **Immediate / no notice** — when you must leave right away (it adds a brief, professional apology for the short notice)
- **Personal reasons** — keeps the why private
- **Retirement** — a warmer close to a career
- **Short & simple** — three sentences when that's all you need
