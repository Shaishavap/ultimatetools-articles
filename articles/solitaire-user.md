## How to Play Solitaire (Klondike Rules)

**Goal:** Move all 52 cards to four foundation piles, one per suit (♠ ♥ ♦ ♣), built up from Ace to King.

**Setup:**
- 7 tableau columns — 1 card in the first, 2 in the second, up to 7 in the last
- Top card in each column is face-up; the rest are face-down
- Remaining cards go to the stock pile (top-left)

**On each turn:**

1. **Move cards between tableau columns** — stack in descending order, alternating color (red on black, black on red). A red 7 goes on a black 8.
2. **Draw from the stock** — click the stock pile to reveal cards in the waste pile. Play from waste to tableau or foundations.
3. **Build foundations** — move Aces to foundations first, then build each suit from Ace → 2 → ... → King.
4. **Empty columns** — only Kings (or a stack starting with a King) can move into an empty tableau column.

**Win:** All four foundations complete (52 cards total).

## Strategy Tips

**Expose face-down cards first.** Your main goal early is to flip hidden cards. Prioritize moves that reveal a new face-down card over moves that look tidy but don't unblock anything.

**Don't rush Aces to foundations early.** If a 3♥ is buried, moving 2♥ to the foundation doesn't help. Keep low cards in the tableau where they may be needed for building sequences.

**Save empty columns for Kings with long stacks.** An empty column is powerful — don't fill it with a lone King that has nothing to build under it.

**Cycle through the stock repeatedly.** A card that was buried may become playable after you've moved things around in the tableau.
