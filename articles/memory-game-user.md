## How to Play

**Goal:** Flip cards two at a time to find matching pairs. Match all pairs to win.

**Rules:**
- Click any face-down card to flip it
- Click a second card — if both cards match, they stay face-up
- If they don't match, both flip back after a short delay
- Continue until all pairs are matched

**Controls:**

| Action | How |
|---|---|
| Flip a card | Click it |
| New game | Click **New Game** |

That's the whole game. The challenge is memory.

---

## What Gets Tracked

- **Moves:** Each pair of flips counts as one move. Fewer moves = better score.
- **Time:** A timer starts on your first flip. Total time shown at completion.
- **Best score:** Your best move count this session is remembered.

---

## Grid Sizes

The game offers different grid sizes to adjust difficulty:

| Grid | Pairs | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| 4×4 | 8 | Easy |
| 4×6 | 12 | Medium |
| 6×6 | 18 | Hard |

Smaller grids are good for a quick game. A 6×6 grid with 18 pairs is a serious test of short-term memory.

---

## Tips

**Build a mental map.** The first few flips are exploratory — you won't know where anything is. Pay attention to every card you see, even the ones you can't match yet.

**Match when you can, remember when you can't.** If you flip a card and you already know where its match is, complete it immediately. If you don't recognize it, remember its position for later.

**Start from one corner.** Working through the grid in a structured pattern helps you remember positions more reliably than clicking randomly.

**Don't rush.** The game rewards memory, not speed. Slower, deliberate plays with fewer moves beats fast, random flipping.
