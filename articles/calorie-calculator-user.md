# Free Calorie Calculator — Find Your TDEE, Daily Target, and Macros

Before you decide how much to eat, you need one number: your **TDEE** — the total calories you burn in a day. Eat below it to lose weight, at it to maintain, above it to gain. ## BMR vs TDEE

- **BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate):** the calories your body burns at complete rest, just keeping you alive.
- **TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure):** your BMR multiplied by an activity factor — the real number you burn including movement and exercise.

This calculator uses the **Mifflin-St Jeor equation**, the most accurate general BMR formula validated in research:

```
Men:   BMR = 10×kg + 6.25×cm − 5×age + 5
Women: BMR = 10×kg + 6.25×cm − 5×age − 161
```

## A real example

**Male, 30, 5'10" (178 cm), 176 lbs (80 kg), lightly active**

- BMR: **1,768 kcal**
- TDEE (maintenance): **2,431 kcal**
- To lose ~1 lb/week: **1,931 kcal** (a 500-calorie deficit)
- To gain ~1 lb/week: **2,931 kcal**

A deficit of about 500 calories a day equals roughly **1 pound (0.45 kg) of fat loss per week** — the sustainable pace most guidelines recommend.

## Macros, not just calories

Calories decide weight; macros decide how you look and feel. The calculator splits your target into protein, carbs and fat. On a 1,931-calorie cut with a balanced split:

- **Protein:** ~145 g
- **Carbs:** ~193 g
- **Fat:** ~64 g

Switch to the **High Protein** preset when cutting to preserve muscle — common targets are 0.7–1 g of protein per pound of bodyweight.
