# How to Play Wordle Online for Free — Daily Word Puzzle, No App, No Login

Wordle became a phenomenon in late 2021 because it nailed one specific thing: one puzzle per day, shared with everyone, solvable in minutes. No levels, no lives, no monetization. Just a word, six tries, and the satisfaction of cracking it before the day ends.

You can play a free version of the daily word puzzle at the [Wordle game](https://ultimatetools.io/tools/fun-tools/wordle/) — new word every day, no account required.

## The rules in one paragraph

You have **6 attempts** to guess a **5-letter word**. After each guess, every letter gets colored:

- **Green** — correct letter, correct position
- **Yellow** — correct letter, wrong position
- **Gray** — letter not in the word at all

Use the colors to eliminate possibilities and zero in on the answer.

## A worked example

Say the word is **CRANE**. You guess **SLATE** first.

```
S → gray   (no S in CRANE)
L → gray   (no L)
A → yellow (A is in CRANE, but not position 3)
T → gray   (no T)
E → green  (E is in position 5 — correct)
```

Second guess: **TRACE**

```
T → gray   (still no T)
R → green  (R is in position 2)
A → green  (A is in position 3)
C → green  (C is in position 4)
E → green  (E in position 5)
```

Third guess: **CRANE** — all green. Solved in 3.

## Strategy: how to pick your opening word

Your first guess should maximize information. You want to cover the most common English letters in one shot. Good starting words:

- **CRANE** — covers C, R, A, N, E (five of the top 10 most frequent letters)
- **SLATE** — S, L, A, T, E
- **AUDIO** — hits all five vowels except E
- **STARE** — S, T, A, R, E

Avoid words with repeated letters in your first guess. Guessing **BOOST** wastes a slot — you could test two different letters instead of confirming one.

## How to use yellow tiles

A yellow tile is just as valuable as a green one — it tells you the letter is in the word and rules out its current position. Don't ignore yellows.

If A comes up yellow in position 2 on your first guess, your next guess should:

1. Contain A somewhere (it's in the word)
2. **Not** have A in position 2 (it doesn't belong there)

Players who ignore yellow tiles tend to repeat the same position for that letter, which wastes a guess.

## The daily puzzle

The word changes every day at midnight, synced to the calendar date. Everyone playing on the same day is solving the same word. That's the reason people share their results — the green/yellow/gray grid pattern is a spoiler-free way to compare how many guesses it took without revealing the answer.

Your result is saved for the day. If you close the tab and come back, the board picks up where you left off.

## What happens if you don't solve it

The word is revealed if you use all 6 guesses without finding it. There's no penalty — just the quiet frustration of seeing an answer that seemed obvious in hindsight. A new puzzle is available the next day.

## Keyboard support

You can type directly on your physical keyboard. Press **Enter** to submit a guess, **Backspace** to delete the last letter. The on-screen keyboard also shows color feedback as you go — letters you've already eliminated turn gray, helping you avoid reusing them.
