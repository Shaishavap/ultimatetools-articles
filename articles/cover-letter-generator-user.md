## The structure every cover letter follows

Whether you have twenty years of experience or none, the shape is the same:

1. **Opening** — the role you're applying for and a hook for why you're a fit
2. **Body** — one or two concrete pieces of evidence that back it up
3. **Close** — a polite ask for an interview

The generator assembles exactly this from your inputs, so you're never staring at a blank page.

## No experience? Lead with transferable proof

"No experience" almost never means "nothing relevant." Pull from:

- **Coursework and projects** — what you built or studied that maps to the job
- **Volunteering and clubs** — teamwork, leadership, reliability
- **Part-time or unrelated work** — showing up, customer skills, ownership
- **Transferable skills** — communication, problem-solving, fast learning

Drop these into the "top skills / experience" box and the draft frames them as evidence you can do the work. The optional AI will only use what you give it — it won't invent a job you never had.
