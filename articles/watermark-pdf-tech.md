## Common Use Cases

**Draft documents:** Add "DRAFT" across all pages before sending for review. Remove the watermark once the document is finalized.

**Confidential files:** Stamp "CONFIDENTIAL" or "INTERNAL USE ONLY" before sharing documents that shouldn't be redistributed.

**Proof copies:** Designers and photographers use watermarks on proof PDFs to prevent unauthorized use before payment.

**Branding:** Add your company name or website URL to PDFs you distribute publicly — whitepapers, reports, guides.

**Preventing reprinting:** A prominent watermark discourages unauthorized copying or redistribution of printed materials.

## How the Watermark Is Applied

The watermark is embedded directly into the PDF page content using pdf-lib. It is not a PDF annotation layer or overlay — it's drawn into the content stream, making it:

- **Permanent** — it cannot be removed by simply deleting an annotation layer
- **Page-wide** — every page gets the watermark, including pages added later if the PDF is merged
- **Print-ready** — the watermark prints exactly as it appears on screen

---

## Privacy: Runs in Your Browser

The watermark is applied entirely in your browser using pdf-lib. Your PDF never leaves your device — it's read locally, modified in memory, and downloaded back to you.

This is important when the PDF contains:
- Legal or financial documents
- Client-facing materials before approval
- Anything with confidential content
