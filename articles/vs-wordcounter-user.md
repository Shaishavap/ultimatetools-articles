# Wordcounter.net Alternative — Word and Character Counter With Reading Time and Keyword Density

Wordcounter.net is one of the oldest and most-used word counting tools online. It works, it is fast, and millions of writers use it daily. But it is also covered in ads and the premium version locks features like keyword density and reading level analysis behind a paywall.

This comparison looks at what Wordcounter.net offers, what it costs, and how [Ultimate Tools Word Counter](https://ultimatetools.io/tools/text-tools/word-counter/) compares for everyday writing and SEO tasks.

---

## What Wordcounter.net offers

Wordcounter.net gives you word count, character count (with and without spaces), and an estimate of reading time. It also offers a keyword density analysis and some readability scores. The interface has a lot of ads. The premium tier ($9.99/month or $47.88/year) removes ads and unlocks additional analysis features.

## Keyword density without a paywall

Keyword density (how often a specific word or phrase appears as a percentage of total words) is useful for SEO writers checking their content optimization. Wordcounter.net shows a basic keyword frequency list on the free tier and gates deeper analysis behind premium.

The [Word Counter](https://ultimatetools.io/tools/text-tools/word-counter/) shows keyword frequency for all words in your text for free -- no premium tier.

## Privacy

Wordcounter.net sends your text to their server for processing. For a blog draft this is fine. For unpublished content, client work, or anything sensitive, this creates an unnecessary copy of your text on a third-party server.

The Word Counter at Ultimate Tools runs in your browser. Your text is never sent anywhere.

## Where Wordcounter.net is better

**Reading level / Flesch-Kincaid** -- Wordcounter.net shows readability scores (Flesch Reading Ease, grade level). This is useful for educational content, legal documents, or content targeting specific audiences. Ultimate Tools does not offer readability scoring.

**Larger community and integrations** -- Wordcounter.net has existed longer and has more integrations and third-party mentions. If you need a tool your team already knows, this is relevant.
