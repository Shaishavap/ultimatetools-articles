# Make Custom Word Bingo Cards Free — AI Themes + Voice Caller, No App

You want a bingo game themed to your event — Christmas words for the family call, baby-shower terms for the party, vocabulary for the classroom — and every option you find is a chore. Printable card packs you have to buy and mail out. Generators where you manually type thirty words into a grid. Apps that make every guest download and sign up. There's a faster path: **type your theme, let AI fill the cards with matching words, share one link, and a built-in voice reads every call aloud.** Free, no app, no login.

Here's how to set it up.

## Numbers, themed words, or your own list

When you create a room you pick a **Card Type**:

- **Numbers** — classic 1–75 bingo
- **Theme (AI)** — type any topic and the cards auto-fill with matching words
- **Custom words** — paste your own list

That second one is the interesting bit, so let's start there.

## Type a theme, AI builds the cards

Choose **Theme (AI)**, type a topic — *Christmas*, *jungle animals*, *office party*, *90s movies*, *fruits and vegetables* — and click **Generate**. You get about thirty themed words back as little chips. Don't like one? Tap the ✕ to drop it. When you're happy, hit **Create Room**, and **every player gets a unique card filled with those words** instead of numbers.

So instead of the host calling "B-12," the host calls "Reindeer," and players dab it on their card. It turns bingo into a game *about* your occasion — which is exactly what makes it land at a holiday dinner, a themed party, or a lesson.

## Or paste your own word list

Prefer full control? Choose **Custom words** and paste your own — one per line or comma-separated. Anything with **24 or more words** works: course vocabulary, the team's inside jokes, guests' names, movie titles, your kid's spelling list. Each player still gets a unique, randomly-generated card from your list.

## The voice caller reads every call aloud

Flip the **Sound** toggle (it's on the host screen *and* every player screen, off by default) and the game speaks each call using your device's voice — the **word** for word bingo, or "**B 12**" for number bingo. Two reasons it's worth turning on:

- **In-person rooms** — the host doesn't have to read the screen out loud; it just announces each call like a real bingo caller.
- **Accessibility & attention** — nobody has to stare at the screen waiting for the next call; they can listen while they dab.

It runs entirely in the browser (the Web Speech API) — nothing to install.

## How to run a game — about 30 seconds

1. **Create a room** — pick your Card Type (Numbers, Theme, or Custom) and a win condition: **Line**, **Full House**, or **Line + Full House**.
2. **Share the room link** — friends open it on any phone, tablet, or laptop, enter a nickname, and instantly get their own unique card.
3. **Call the items** — manually, or turn on **Auto-Call** (every 3, 5, or 10 seconds). Turn on **Sound** and each call is read aloud.
4. **Players mark and claim** — tap called squares; when a winning pattern is complete, hit **BINGO!** and the server verifies it.

## A few practical notes

- **No app, no login** — it runs in any modern browser; players just open the link.
- **Unique cards every time** — randomly generated, so no two players share a card.
- **Refreshing is safe** — your card and your marked squares are saved in your browser, so an accidental refresh drops you right back where you were.
- **Rooms last a few hours**, and the host can start a fresh round in the same room any time — players don't need to rejoin.
