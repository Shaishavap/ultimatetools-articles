## Why it works

A thank-you note does three things at once: it shows good manners, keeps you top of mind while the decision is being made, and gives you one more chance to reaffirm your fit. Sent within 24 hours, it's a small effort that quietly separates you from candidates who didn't bother.

## What to include (keep it short)

Three or four sentences is ideal:

1. Thank them for their time
2. Reaffirm your interest in the role
3. Reference one specific moment from the conversation
4. Close with a forward-looking line

That's the whole thing. Longer is not better — a tight note reads as confident.

## Email or letter?

After most interviews, **email** is the norm because it arrives fast. The wording is identical either way: write it here, then copy the body into your email (subject line: "Thank you — [Role] interview") or attach the PDF. The generator handles both.

## Situations it covers

- **After a first interview** — the standard follow-up
- **After a phone screen** — shorter, lighter, confirms enthusiasm for the next step
- **After a second / final interview** — more specific; reference the team or a project discussed
- **Short thank-you note** — three sentences when that's all that's needed
