## Why It Adds a Shadow Automatically

Here's a small detail that matters: the tool adds a subtle shadow behind your watermark text.

If your watermark is white and your image has a bright sky, the text would disappear. The auto-shadow creates contrast so the watermark stays readable regardless of the background.

If you pick a dark watermark color, the shadow switches to light. It adapts automatically.

Right now, the tool supports text watermarks. If you need to overlay a logo image, that's a different workflow — typically requires layering two images.

## Can Someone Remove It?

Honestly? If the watermark is small and in the corner, yes — a crop removes it. That's why position and size matter.

For better protection:

- Use center position at moderate opacity
- Increase the font size
- Use a lower opacity (30–40%) so it blends in but is still detectable

No watermark is foolproof. But a visible watermark deters casual theft, which is the majority of cases.
