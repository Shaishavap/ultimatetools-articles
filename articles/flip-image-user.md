## Flip vs Rotate — What's the Difference?

These two operations look similar but do different things:

**Flip (mirror)** — creates a mirror image along an axis. The content is reflected, not turned.
- Flip horizontal = left side becomes right side (like looking in a mirror)
- Flip vertical = top becomes bottom (like turning a page upside down)

**Rotate** — turns the image by a set number of degrees around its center.
- 90° clockwise, 180°, 270° — the image turns but is not mirrored

A selfie taken on a front camera needs a **horizontal flip** — the camera mirrors your face, so the result looks reversed. Rotating it 180° would turn it upside down, not fix the mirror effect.

## When to Flip Horizontal

**Selfies and front-camera photos** — front cameras mirror the preview so you look natural while framing the shot. The saved image is already corrected on most phones, but some apps save the mirrored version. Flip horizontal to fix it.

**Logos and icons** — a logo facing left needs to face right for a layout. Flip it rather than recreating it.

**Text in images** — if scanned text or a sign appears backwards, a horizontal flip corrects it.

**Profile photos** — some layouts look better when the subject faces a specific direction. Flip to change which way they're looking without reshooting.

---

## When to Flip Vertical

**Upside-down scans** — documents scanned face-down on a flatbed sometimes come out upside down. A vertical flip (or 180° rotation) corrects them.

**Reflection effects** — creating a water reflection or shadow effect by flipping the image vertically and placing it below the original.

**Inverted UI assets** — some icon sets expect a specific orientation; flipping vertically repositions them without editing the source file.
