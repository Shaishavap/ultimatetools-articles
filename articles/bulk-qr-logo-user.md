## The catch with logos — and how it's handled

A logo covers part of the QR code, so without enough redundancy the code can stop scanning. QR codes have four **error-correction levels** — L (~7%), M (~15%), Q (~25%), H (~30%) — that control how much of the code can be obscured and still read.

When you add a logo, the tool automatically switches to **level H** and keeps the logo to about 40% of the centre, so your codes stay reliably scannable. You can also set the error-correction level yourself if you're printing very small or expecting wear.
