## The Most Important Mechanic: Paddle Angle Control

Most players treat the paddle like a flat wall — position it under the ball and it bounces back. That's correct, but incomplete.

**Where the ball hits the paddle determines where it goes next:**

- Center of paddle → ball returns at roughly the same angle it came from
- Left edge → ball deflects sharply to the left
- Right edge → ball deflects sharply to the right

This means you can steer the ball. Not just keep it alive — actually aim it at specific bricks.

Once this clicks, the game changes. You stop playing defensively (keep the ball from falling) and start playing offensively (direct the ball toward the section you want to clear next).

## Clear the Bottom Bricks Early

Counter-intuitive for new players: target the bottom rows of bricks before the top ones.

Here's why: after the upper bricks are gone, the ball has a clear path to the top wall and comes back down. Any surviving lower bricks are now hard to reach — the ball hits the top, bounces around the ceiling, and falls straight back without touching the stranded lower rows.

**Early in a level, angle the ball downward toward bottom-row bricks.** Yes, the top bricks are easier to hit. But clearing a path to the bottom first gives you access to the whole board.

## Create a Roof-Run

One of the most satisfying mechanics in Brick Breaker: when the ball gets trapped between the top wall and the top row of bricks, it bounces rapidly and clears an entire row without you doing anything.

To set this up:
1. Clear the brick directly adjacent to a wall at the top row
2. Angle the ball into the gap you've created
3. The ball enters the space above the bricks and bounces off the ceiling, clearing brick after brick on its way across

Deliberately creating a gap to exploit isn't just satisfying — it's the fastest way to clear a dense upper section.

## The Lives Resource

You start with 3 lives. Losing a life doesn't just hurt emotionally — it limits your options going forward. Every life preserved is a cushion for the harder patterns in later levels.

**Don't accept lost lives as inevitable.** Most ball losses happen in the first three seconds after a ball drops, when the player is repositioning from chasing the ball across the screen. The fix: **watch the ball's angle after it bounces off a brick, not after it passes the midpoint of the screen.** You have more reaction time if you start moving the paddle earlier.

## Dealing With Isolated Bricks

The hardest scenario: one brick remaining in the upper-left corner of the board after everything else is cleared. The ball can only reach it by deflecting off two walls in sequence.

To reach a corner brick:
1. Position the paddle slightly off-center toward the corner the brick is in
2. Let the ball angle naturally up toward that corner
3. Don't try to force it with hard edge hits — a moderate angle toward the wall sequence is more consistent than a sharp redirect

Patience here beats force. The ball will reach it — don't sacrifice a life trying to speed it up.

## Score Optimization

Every brick destroyed adds 10 points. Level completion adds a bonus.

The math: clearing a level quickly with all lives intact earns more than clearing it slowly after losing two lives. **The bonus multiplier is real — preserve lives.**

On harder levels where bricks are dense, the highest-value play is often to slow down the ball angle deliberately — take fewer risks with edge hits, accept some lost positions — in exchange for completing the level without losing a life.

---

## Summary: The Three Principles

**Aim, don't just bounce.** Use edge hits to steer the ball. Every shot is steerable.

**Clear bottom first.** Stranded bricks at the bottom are harder than they look once the top opens up.

**Lives are resources.** Play conservatively when ahead; be aggressive only when you can afford to lose one.
