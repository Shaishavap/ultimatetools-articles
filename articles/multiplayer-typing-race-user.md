## How the Multiplayer Typing Race Works

On the typing test page, click **Create a Race**. You become the host: pick a nickname, and you get a private room link. Share that link however you like — WhatsApp, Slack, a group chat — and anyone who opens it joins your room with their own nickname.

Once everyone's in the lobby, the host hits **Start Race**. A synced 5-second countdown runs on every screen, then the same passage appears for all racers at once. As you type, you see:

- **Live progress bars** for every racer, updating as they go
- **Each person's WPM** ticking up in real time
- A **finish-order leaderboard** — first to type the passage correctly wins, with WPM as the tiebreaker

When you finish, you instantly see your placement; slower racers keep going for their spot. The host can start a fresh round with a new passage anytime.

## Why Racing Beats a Solo Typing Test

A solo WPM test tells you a number. A race gives you a *reason to push*. The moment you see someone else's bar pull ahead, you type faster than you thought you could — competition does what willpower alone usually can't.

It's also just more fun, which is the part that actually matters for practice. People quit solo typing tutors within a week. Nobody quits a game they're losing to their coworker by 3 WPM. A quick race is the perfect team warm-up, classroom activity, or "settle it once and for all" challenge.

## Set Up a Race in 30 Seconds

1. Open the [typing speed test](https://ultimatetools.io/tools/fun-tools/typing-speed-test/) and click **Create a Race**.
2. Enter a nickname and join your own room.
3. **Copy the room link** and send it to whoever you want to race.

5. Hit **Start Race** — countdown, then type. Fastest finisher wins.

## How It Works (and One Honest Note)

Rooms are stored server-side as lightweight files and clients poll for updates roughly every couple of seconds — so opponent progress bars update every ~2s, not at 60fps. That's smooth enough for a casual typing race and keeps it fast and account-free. It's built for fun and bragging rights, not esports-grade frame-perfect sync. Your typed text never leaves your browser except as a simple progress percentage and WPM number.

After a race, hit **Share Result** to generate a small card showing your placement and WPM — drop it in the group chat as proof.
