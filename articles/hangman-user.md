## How the Game Works

A word is chosen at random from the selected category. Every letter in the word is shown as a blank. You have **6 wrong guesses** before the hangman is complete.

Guess letters by clicking the on-screen keyboard or pressing keys on your physical keyboard. Each correct guess reveals the letter in the word. Each wrong guess adds one body part to the gallows — head, body, left arm, right arm, left leg, right leg.

- **Guess all letters before the figure is complete:** you win
- **6 wrong guesses:** the figure is complete, the word is revealed, start again

Correctly guessed letters turn green. Wrong guesses turn gray and dim. You can't accidentally guess the same letter twice.

## Strategy: What to Guess First

Start with the most common English letters: **E, T, A, O, I, N**. Most English words contain at least one of these, so you're likely to reveal something early.

Category-specific tips:
- **Countries:** A and I are almost always present. Avoid Q, X, Z in the first few guesses.
- **Tech:** T, I, O, N are common. Many tech words end in -TION, -ER, -ING, -ION.
- **Animals:** Double letters are common (RACCOON, FLAMINGO). If you reveal one, guess it again.

## Why 6 Wrong Guesses?

The six-guess limit comes from the classic pen-and-paper version — six body parts to draw: head, body, two arms, two legs. It gives enough chances to work through a word without making it too easy. A short 5-letter word is manageable; a 9-letter word with uncommon letters is a real challenge.
