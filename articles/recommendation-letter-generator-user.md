## The three-part structure

Every strong recommendation follows the same arc:

1. **Who and how** — who you're recommending and your relationship to them
2. **Why** — specific strengths, backed by a concrete example
3. **The endorsement** — a clear, unreserved recommendation and your contact details

The generator builds this from a few fields, so the letter is coherent before you change a word.

## Recommendation vs reference letter

They're nearly the same thing. A reference letter is often a bit more general; a recommendation is tied to a specific opportunity. This tool handles both — just pick whether it's for a **job**, **university / grad school**, or a **character reference**, and the wording adjusts.

## Common situations it covers

- **For a student, from a teacher** — set your title to Professor/Teacher and the university purpose; it's framed for an admissions committee
- **For an employee, from a manager** — speaks to a future employer about role, impact, and reliability
- **Grad school admission** — leans on potential, curiosity, and growth
- **Character reference** — for a tenancy, court, or community role; focuses on integrity over job skills
