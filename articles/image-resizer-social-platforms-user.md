# Image Size Guide for Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn & YouTube — Resize Free in Browser

Resizing images to the right pixel dimensions before uploading to Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, or YouTube prevents cropping, stretching, and platform-side compression artifacts. Every social platform has different requirements. This is the reference guide for the sizes that actually matter — and how to resize to exact pixel dimensions free, without leaving your browser.

## Why Platform Sizes Matter

Social platforms don't passively display your image. They process it:

- Images that don't match the expected ratio get **auto-cropped** — usually from the edges
- Images smaller than recommended get **upscaled** — adding visible blur
- Images dramatically larger than needed get **resampled** — losing sharpness in unpredictable ways

Getting the dimensions right before upload means the platform displays exactly what you designed.

## Image Sizes by Platform

### Instagram

| Format | Dimensions | Ratio |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Square post | 1080×1080px | 1:1 |
| Portrait post | 1080×1350px | 4:5 |
| Landscape post | 1080×566px | 1.91:1 |
| Story / Reel cover | 1080×1920px | 9:16 |
| Profile picture | 320×320px (displayed at 110px) | 1:1 |

Instagram crops portrait images to 4:5 in the feed. For full control, shoot or crop to 4:5 (1080×1350) before uploading. Landscape is rarely cropped.

### Twitter / X

| Format | Dimensions | Ratio |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| In-feed image | 1200×675px | 16:9 |
| In-feed tall crop | 1200×628px | 1.91:1 |
| Profile header | 1500×500px | 3:1 |
| Profile picture | 400×400px | 1:1 |
| Card image (og:image) | 1200×628px | 1.91:1 |

Twitter's feed crops images dynamically. A safe zone is to keep the important content in the **centre 600×400px** area. For OG/card images linked from articles, 1200×628 is the standard.

### LinkedIn

| Format | Dimensions | Ratio |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Post image | 1200×627px | 1.91:1 |
| Article cover image | 1200×627px | 1.91:1 |
| Company page banner | 1128×191px | ~6:1 |
| Profile background | 1584×396px | 4:1 |
| Profile picture | 400×400px | 1:1 |
| Shared link preview | 1200×627px | 1.91:1 |

LinkedIn's feed heavily favours 1.91:1. Posts with that ratio display edge-to-edge in the feed — portrait images get thumbnail treatment. Use 1200×627 for maximum impact.

### YouTube

| Format | Dimensions | Ratio |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Thumbnail | 1280×720px | 16:9 |
| Channel art (desktop) | 2560×1440px | 16:9 |
| Channel art (safe zone) | 1546×423px | centred area |
| Profile picture | 800×800px | 1:1 |
| End screen overlay | 1280×720px | 16:9 |

YouTube thumbnails are one of the highest-leverage images on the platform — they're your click rate. Use the full 1280×720 at high quality and keep text in the centre 70% of the frame.

### Facebook

| Format | Dimensions | Ratio |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Feed post image | 1200×630px | 1.91:1 |
| Feed square image | 1080×1080px | 1:1 |
| Cover photo (page) | 851×315px | 2.7:1 |
| Cover photo (group) | 1640×856px | ~1.91:1 |
| Profile picture | 170×170px | 1:1 |
| Event cover | 1920×1005px | ~1.91:1 |

Facebook's algorithm gives larger display area to 1:1 and 4:5 images in the mobile feed. For link-share cards, 1200×630 is standard.

## Aspect Ratio vs Dimensions

Most platforms care more about **aspect ratio** than exact pixel count. 1200×627 and 2400×1254 are both 1.91:1 — both work. But stick close to the recommended pixel count:

- **Too small** → platform upscales → blur
- **Too large** → platform downscales → acceptable but wastes upload time

A practical rule: stay within 50% of the recommended pixel count in either direction.
