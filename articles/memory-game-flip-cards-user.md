## How the Game Works

This is a classic card flip memory game. Every card has a hidden emoji symbol on its face. Cards are dealt face-down in a grid. On each turn:

1. Click any face-down card — it flips to reveal its emoji
2. Click a second card — it flips too
3. If the symbols match, both cards stay face-up (matched)
4. If they don't match, both cards flip back face-down after a brief pause

The goal: match every pair on the board in as few moves as possible.

The "brief pause" before unmatched cards flip back is the mechanic the whole game depends on. You get just enough time to register what you saw — then it's gone. Remembering those positions is the skill.

## Move Counter and Timer

Every round tracks two things:

**Moves** — each time you flip a second card and compare a pair, the move counter increments by one. Lower is better. A "perfect" Easy round takes 4 moves (find every pair on the first try — essentially impossible). A realistic good Easy round is 7–10 moves.

**Timer** — starts the moment you flip your first card, stops when you match the last pair. The goal is to improve both numbers simultaneously. Rushing to flip faster usually increases your move count; playing carefully and slow reduces moves but increases time.

The tension between those two metrics is what makes card flip matching games worth replaying.

## Tips for a Better Score

**Build a mental map before you flip.** On Easy and Medium, spend 2–3 seconds scanning the full grid before you click anything. There's no time penalty for thinking. On Hard, this matters even more.

**Flip unknown cards near previously seen ones.** If you remember seeing a star emoji in the top-right, and you need to flip a new card anyway, pick one that's near a card you haven't seen yet — maximizing new information per move.

**Use revealed-but-unmatched cards as anchors.** When a pair gets flipped and doesn't match, both positions become known. Treat those positions as resources — when you eventually flip the matching card for one of them, you know exactly where to go.

**Don't rush after a miss.** After non-matching cards flip back, pause briefly and mentally note where they were. Players who click immediately after a miss tend to forget the position they just saw.
